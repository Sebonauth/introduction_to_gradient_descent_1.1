Introduction to Gradient Descent

Imagine you're trying to find the lowest point in a valley, but you're blindfolded. You can only feel the slope of the ground beneath your feet. How would you do it? You'd probably take small steps downhill, constantly feeling for the steepest descent. That's the basic idea behind Gradient Descent!

Continue

In machine learning, we often deal with complex functions called cost functions that represent the error of our model. Our goal is to find the input values that minimize this error. Gradient Descent is an algorithm that helps us do this by iteratively adjusting the model's parameters.

Build Your Vocab

Cost Function

A function that measures the error of a machine learning model. Lower cost means less error.

Continue

Gradient Descent works by taking steps proportional to the negative of the gradient of the cost function. The gradient tells us the direction of the steepest ascent. By taking the negative, we move in the direction of the steepest descent – towards the minimum.

Build Your Vocab

Gradient

The vector of partial derivatives of a function. It points in the direction of the greatest rate of increase of the function.

Think of it like this: the steeper the slope, the larger the step you'll take downhill.

Continue

How big of a step should we take? That's controlled by a parameter called the learning rate (α). A small learning rate means small steps, ensuring we don't overshoot the minimum. A large learning rate means large steps, potentially leading to faster convergence but also a risk of oscillation or divergence.

Build Your Vocab

Learning Rate (α)

A hyperparameter that controls the step size in Gradient Descent. It determines how quickly the algorithm learns.

Choosing the right learning rate is crucial for successful Gradient Descent.
